As it was mentioned in our Cooking gas shortage section, Venezuela has the world's largest oil reserves, and even though, during the first semester of 2020, Venezuelans have been suffering a terrible fuel shortage.

On April 27, 2020, DW News reported on Youtube: "Venezuela has the largest oil reserves in the world, so it comes as a surprise to some that gasoline has been in short supply there for years. Without fuel it could be easier to keep to coronavirus lockdown regulations, but at what cost? The shortage has now reached unsustainable levels, and is hitting the capital Caracas in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic".

People in Venezuela spend up to 4 days (yes, days) in a queue just to refill their fuel tank. The worst part is that when it's finally their turn, there might not be any fuel left for them.

To the right of this paragraph, you can watch a video where one of those endless queues is shown. And in the mean time, Venezuela's PDVSA steps up fuel shipments to Cuba.

We remind our readers that the oil industry was nationalized. The Oild Industry, just like the rest, was destroyed, and one of the main reasons of the fuel shortage is thay none of Venezuela's refineries are operating.
