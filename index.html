The facts


Venezuela is known as a country rich in natural resources. In fact, according to different sources, as Investopedia and Worldatlas, Venezuela is part of the 10 countries with the most natural resources in the whole world, ranked as eighth on both sites.

In spite of that, Venezuelans are living the worst economic and social crisis in their entire history.

Such crisis is really complex, however, some of its main causes are:


  • The corruption: Venezuela is the 173th least corrupt nation out of 180 countries, according to the 2019 Corruption Perceptions Index reported by Transparency International.

  • The industries nationalization: The main industries of the country were nationalized, including:

    Hugo Chavez expropriating buildings full of private shops.

    • The Oil Industry
    • The Steel Industry
    • Agriculture
    • Banking
    • Gold Mining
    • Telecomms
    • Electricity
    • Turism and Travel
    • Transporation

    Source: Foundation for Economic Education and Reuters.

  • The price controls: There is plenty information from trustworthy sources about this topic, including:
    • "Price controls and scarcity force Venezuelans to turn to the black market for milk and toilet paper." By The Guardian.
    • "Venezuela Announces Price Controls as Food Shortages Worsen." By Bloomberg.
    • "Venezuela imposes price controls again to limit accelerating inflation." By Reuters.
    • "Venezuela sets new price controls, with eggs costing more than a month's wages." By Reuters.

These, among other facts, have caused a tremendous degradation of the quality of life for the Venezuelans. Even though, please keep in mind that we are only scratching the surface of how living in Venezuela is.
