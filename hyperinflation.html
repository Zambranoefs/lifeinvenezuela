Imagine that today you go to a convenience store near your house to buy some snacks, maybe you want to eat them while watching a movie or TV show with your family during the evening. You spent 2 dollars on them. The next day you go to the same convenience store to buy the exact same products, however this time you spent 3 dollars on them. The next day, 4 dollars, the next day, 5 dollars, so on and so forth, until you decide to stop buying them because such products have become a luxury for you, you can't afford them anymore.

This is a simple example of how is to live under an hyperinflationary economy like Venezuela's, where by January 2020 the minimum wage would pay for less than 1 kilogram of beef. Recently, since May 1, 2020, the new minimum wage totalizes aproximately 4.66$. Bear in mind though, that those 4.66$ include 2.33$ that correspond to meal allowance, so the base salary for someone who earns a minimum wage, is actually 2.33$.

But how do Venezuelans survive? Might the reader ask.

By November 2019, more than 4.5 million Venezuelans had emigrated around the globe. Most of those Venezuelans, like me, send money home, so that their families can survive. However, not everyone in Venezuela have a family member abroad. Many are dying of hunger, and babies are dying of malnutrition.
